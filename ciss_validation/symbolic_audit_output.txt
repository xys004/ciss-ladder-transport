=== Inter-site coupling block C ===
\left[\begin{matrix}V & D_{u}\\D_{d} & V\end{matrix}\right]
Matrix([[V, D_u], [D_d, V]])

=== C C^dagger ===
Matrix([[D_u*D_u_conj + V**2, V*(D_d_conj + D_u)], [V*(D_d + D_u_conj), D_d*D_d_conj + V**2]])
LaTeX:
\left[\begin{matrix}D_{u} D_{u conj} + V^{2} & V \left(D_{d conj} + D_{u}\right)\\V \left(D_{d} + D_{u conj}\right) & D_{d} D_{d conj} + V^{2}\end{matrix}\right]

Off-diagonal Schur/interference terms:
(C C†)_{up,down} = V*(D_d_conj + D_u)
(C C†)_{down,up} = V*(D_d + D_u_conj)

=== C C^dagger under TRS condition D_down = -D_up^* ===
Matrix([[D_u*D_u_conj + V**2, 0], [0, D_u*D_u_conj + V**2]])
LaTeX:
\left[\begin{matrix}D_{u} D_{u conj} + V^{2} & 0\\0 & D_{u} D_{u conj} + V^{2}\end{matrix}\right]

Conclusion:
Under TRS, off-diagonal spin mixing generated by Schur decimation cancels exactly.
When D_down != -D_up^*, this cancellation is lost and the full 4x4 Green function is required.

=== Exact site-1 Schur denominator A - C B^{-1} C† ===
Matrix([[(-D_u*D_u_conj - V**2 + a1*a2)/a2, V*(-D_d_conj - D_u)/a2], [V*(-D_d - D_u_conj)/a2, (-D_d*D_d_conj - V**2 + a1*a2)/a2]])
LaTeX:
\left[\begin{matrix}\frac{- D_{u} D_{u conj} - V^{2} + a_{1} a_{2}}{a_{2}} & \frac{V \left(- D_{d conj} - D_{u}\right)}{a_{2}}\\\frac{V \left(- D_{d} - D_{u conj}\right)}{a_{2}} & \frac{- D_{d} D_{d conj} - V^{2} + a_{1} a_{2}}{a_{2}}\end{matrix}\right]

=== Schur denominator under TRS ===
Matrix([[(-D_u*D_u_conj - V**2 + a1*a2)/a2, 0], [0, (-D_u*D_u_conj - V**2 + a1*a2)/a2]])
LaTeX:
\left[\begin{matrix}\frac{- D_{u} D_{u conj} - V^{2} + a_{1} a_{2}}{a_{2}} & 0\\0 & \frac{- D_{u} D_{u conj} - V^{2} + a_{1} a_{2}}{a_{2}}\end{matrix}\right]

In the TRS limit, each diagonal element has the form
    a1 - (V^2 + |D_up|^2)/a2
which is the decoupled-channel expression used in the analytical limit.
